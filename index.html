Detection logic (coronal plane only): Lateral head tilt is reported as an angle from vertical and flagged above 15°, with a severe band above 30°; the reading is capped at 45°, the approximate limit of cervical lateral flexion. Shoulder asymmetry is the vertical difference between the shoulders normalised by shoulder width, with a threshold calibrated from still-neutral self-data. Trunk lean fires when the shoulder-to-hip line deviates more than 12° from vertical, and reports N/A when the hips are not visible — which is most of the time at a desk. A capture-geometry gate rejects frames when the user is out of range or the camera is tilted, and every rejection is recorded with its reason. A micro-break is suggested after 25 minutes of continuous detected sitting (presence-gated). Scope: a front-on monocular camera cannot observe sagittal-axis motion, so forward head posture, lumbar lordosis and true scapular protraction are out of scope — only coronal-plane lateral tilt and shoulder asymmetry are measured.
